It started off as an idea that came in the middle of the night.


Nine years ago when the idea of a musical history of San Francisco came to me in the middle of the night, little did I know what I was in for. After scribbling down over 20 movement titles, I eventually settled on 14, and began working more or less in chronological order on each one.


Dancing on the Brink of the World is the the name of an Ohlone song to which the words and music have been lost in time. Not only does the title reflect what it must have been like for the Bay Areaâ€™s original people who were all but destroyed by the arrival of the Europeans, but as history unfolded, the little village of Yerba Buena that became San Francisco also became the new Brink of the World. The Ohlone, Mission Dolores, the Gold Rush, Chinatown, the Earthquake and Fire, Golden Gate Park, the Golden Gate Bridge, the Beatniks, the Psychedelic Scene, the Gay Movement. The music tells these stories.


We begin 400 years ago when there were just a few small Ohlone villages scattered around the edge of the San Francisco bay.

Kickstarter Campaign

$8,100.00

In late 2012, we began a Kickstarter campaign to raise the funds for the upcoming 2013 premiere of Dancing on the Brink of the World. Collin Boltz, who was in charge of the SF Conservatory of Music festival where concert would be taking place, introduced me to Louis Cruz, another student of my teacher David Conte. Louis and I met early one morning and spent a memorable day shooting video in and around San Francisco, including some great footage driving across the Golden Gate Bridge. Louis then added some musical clips from past recordings of the individual movements, and completed the Kickstarter video, which was successful in helping us reach our goal of $7,500.
